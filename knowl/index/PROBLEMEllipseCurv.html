Problem 6.107.

Consider the ellipse given by \(\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1\text{,}\) \(a\gt b\gt 0\text{.}\)
(a)
Show that the curvature is given by \(\kappa=\frac{a^4b^4}{(a^4y^2+b^4x^2)^{\frac32}}\text{.}\)
Hint.
Rewrite \(\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1\) as \(b^2x^2+a^2y^2=a^2b^2\text{.}\)
(b)
Looking at this graph it appears that the curvature at \((a,0)\) should be greater than at \((0,b)\text{.}\) Use the formula you just derived to verify this.
(c)
A circle is the special case of an ellipse when \(a=b\text{.}\) Use the formula from partÂ 6.107.a to compute the curvature of a circle.
