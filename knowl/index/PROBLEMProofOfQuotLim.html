Problem 17.86.
(a)
Prove that if \(\limit{x}{\infty}{f(x)}=L_f\) and \(\limit{x}{\infty}{g(x)}=L_g\neq0\) then
\begin{equation*} \limit{x}{\infty}{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}=\frac{L_f}{L_g}. \end{equation*}
Hint.
Rewrite \(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) as \(f(x)\cdot\inverse{(g(x))}\text{.}\) Which of our theorems can you apply?
(b)
Prove that if \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{f(x)}=L_f\) and \(\limit{x}{-\infty}{g(x)}=L_g\neq0\) then
\begin{equation*} \limit{x}{-\infty}{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}=\frac{L_f}{L_g}. \end{equation*}
(c)
Prove that if \(a\) is some real number, and \(\limit{x}{a}{f(x)}=L_f\text{,}\) and \(\limit{x}{a}{g(x)}=L_g\neq0\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \limit{x}{a}{\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}=\frac{L_f}{L_g}. \end{equation*}
