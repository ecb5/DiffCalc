Problem 4.59.
Assume that neither \(x\) nor \(y\) is zero. Show that each of the following statements is true.
(a)
The Product Rule can be rearranged as:
\begin{equation*} \dx\left(xy\right) = xy\left(\frac{\dx{x}}{x}+\frac{\dx{y}}{y}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
(b)
The Quotient Rule can be rearranged as:
\begin{equation*} \dx\left(\frac{x}{y}\right) = \frac{x}{y}\left(\frac{\dx{x}}{x}-\frac{\dx{y}}{y}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
(c)
Why must we assume that neither \(x\) nor \(y\) is zero?
