By the Principle of Local Linearity, the line tangent to the curve and the curve itself are going to be nearly indistinguishable near to the point \((0,1)\text{.}\) Moreover, we can find any point on the tangent line since we have its equation. If we increment the \(x\)-coordinate just a little bit while staying on the tangent line then the corresponding \(y-\)coordinate on the tangent line will be close to the \(y-\)coordinate on the curve.
