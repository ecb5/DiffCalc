In your earlier math courses you may have seen graphs that looked like this before. If not, then consider the following graphs of the exponential functions, \(y=2^x\) and \(y=3^x\) and notice that they are very similar to the graph of \(y= 1+x+\frac{x^2}{2!}+\frac{x^3}{3!}+ \cdots + \frac{x^{50}}{50!}\text{.}\)
