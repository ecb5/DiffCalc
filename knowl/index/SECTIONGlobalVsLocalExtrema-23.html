In the sketch above \(y(a)\) is the global minimum of \(y\) since it is the lowest point on the graph of \(y(x)\text{,}\) whereas \(y(c)\) is a local minimum because it is the lowest point on the graph when the domain of \(y(x)\) is restricted to the interval \((c-\eps, c+\eps)\) (between the dashed lines). In this sketch the global maximum of \(y(x)\) occurs at the endpoints of the interval, \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\text{.}\) The fact that extrema can also occur at endpoints is another complicating detail we will come back to shortly.
