Problem 5.70.
Let \((x,y)\) represent any point on the Folium \(x^3+y^3=3xy\) which is not the origin. Let \(t\) represent the slope of the line joining the origin to \((x,y)\text{;}\) that is, \(\frac{y}{x}=t\) or \(y=tx\text{.}\) Use this to show that
\begin{equation*} x=\frac{3t}{1+t^3},\text{ and } y=\frac{3t^2}{1+t^3}. \end{equation*}
The difference between this problem and Problem 5.69 is this: In Problem 5.69 we gave you \(x(t)\) and \(y(t)\) and asked you to show that it satisfies the equation of the Folium. Here we start with the equation of the Folium and you need to find \(x(t)\) and \(y(t)\text{.}\)
