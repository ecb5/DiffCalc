To use a table of base \(10\) logarithms to find the product of two numbers we would first look up the logarithm of each number, then add the logarithms. The resulting sum is the base \(10\) logarithm of the product, so we’d look up in our table what this sum is the logarithm of. The result is the product of the two original numbers. Since the invention of modern computing technology the original purpose of base \(10\) logarithms — simplifying numerical computations — is completely obsolete. So tables of base \(10\) logarithms
