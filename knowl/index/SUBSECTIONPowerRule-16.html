Problem 4.28.
(a)
Show that the two triangles in Figure 4.27 are similar. Use this to show that
\begin{equation*} y\dx{y}=-x\dx{x}. \end{equation*}
Notice that this problem specifically asks you to use the triangles, not differentiation to show that \(y\dx{y}=-x\dx{x}.\)
(b)
After Problem 4.15 we observed that the ratio \(\dfdx{y}{x}\) must represent the slope of both the curve and its tangent line at a given point. Notice that the differential equation for the circle can be written as
\begin{equation*} \dfdx{y}{x}=-\frac{x}{y}. \end{equation*}
Explain how this is related to the geometric fact that the tangent to a circle at a point is perpendicular to the radius through that point.
