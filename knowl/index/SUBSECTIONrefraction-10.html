Physically, what Part 9.22.d says is that when you are beneath the surface of a pond and look upward the entire view of the world above the surface is compressed into a circle at the top of a cone with central angle \(\beta\text{.}\) Attempts to look beyond that angle will result in you seeing a (probably dark) reflection from the bottom of the pond as no light that enters from above and outside this circle will reach your eye at point \(P\text{.}\) This phenomenon is called Snell’s Window and is illustrated in the image below.
