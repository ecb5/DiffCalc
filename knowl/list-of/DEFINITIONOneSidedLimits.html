Definition 16.1.0.12. One–sided Limits.

If \(R\) is a real number (or \(-\infty\) or \(\infty\)) and
\begin{equation*} \rlimit{x}{a}{f(x)} = R \end{equation*}
we say that \(R\) is the right–hand limit of \(f(x)\) at \(x=a\text{.}\) If \(L\) is a real number (or \(-\infty\) or \(\infty\)) and
\begin{equation*} \llimit{x}{a}{f(x)} = L \end{equation*}
we say that \(L\) is the left–hand limit of \(f(x)\) at \(x=a\text{.}\)
