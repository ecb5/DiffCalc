Example 9.5.12. Constructing a Square on a Parabola, Redux.

Given our new understanding of the distinction between local and global extrema we will revisit ExampleÂ 9.4.1. The objective function is still \(A = (x-2)^2 + (y-3)^2 \) so when we substitute the constraint, \(y = x^2\text{,}\) we get the abstract problem of finding the lowest points on the graph of
\begin{equation*} A(x) = (x-2)^2 + (x^2-3)^2 \end{equation*}
Solving \(\dfdx{A}{x}=0\) gives us three POTPs: \(x \approx-1.32\text{,}\) \(x \approx-0.43\text{,}\) and \(x \approx1.75\) as before, so the intervals we need are, \((-\infty, -1.32)\text{,}\) \((-1.32, -0.43)\text{,}\) \((-0.43, 1.75)\text{,}\) and \((1.75, \infty)\text{.}\) Because our POTPs are approximations these intervals are also approximate.
