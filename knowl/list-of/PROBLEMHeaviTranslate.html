Problem 16.2.6.

(a)

Let \(H(x)\) be the Heaviside function and let \(a\) be a real number.
  1. Sketch \(H(x-a)\text{.}\)
  2. Use Theorem 16.1.13 to show that \(H(x-a)\) is not differentiable at \(x=a\text{.}\)

(b)

Let \(b\) also be a real number, distinct from \(a\text{,}\) and define
\begin{equation*} f(x) =H(x-a) + H(x-b). \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch \(f(x)\text{.}\)
  2. Use Theorem 16.1.13 to show that is not differentiable at \(x=a\) or \(x=b\text{.}\)
