Problem 7.2.12. Find the Pattern.

(a)
Apply Newton’s Method to the function \(f(x)=x^2-a\text{,}\) shown above, to determine the iteration formula for approximating \(\sqrt{a}\text{.}\) Show that this is precisely the \((n+1)\)st iteration you get when using the Babylonian method:
\begin{equation*} r_{n+1}=\frac{1}{2} \left(r_n+\frac{a}{r_n} \right). \end{equation*}
(b)
Use Newton’s Method on the function \(f(x)=x^3-a\) to obtain the iteration formula: \(\displaystyle r_{n+1}=\frac{1}{3} \left(2r_n+\frac{a}{r_n^2} \right)\) for approximating \(\sqrt[3]{a}\text{.}\) Notice that \(\frac{1}{3} \left(2r_n+\frac{a}{r_n^2} \right)\) is the average of \(r_n, r_n\text{,}\) and \(\frac{a}{r_n^2}\text{.}\)
(c)
Obtain the iteration scheme \(\displaystyle r_{n+1}=\frac{1}{4} \left(3r_n+\frac{a}{r_n^3} \right)\) for approximating the fourth root of \(a.\) Is this also an average? Of what?
(d)
Now find an iteration scheme to find the \(k\)th root of \(a,\) if \(a\) is a positive number, and \(k\) is a positive integer.
