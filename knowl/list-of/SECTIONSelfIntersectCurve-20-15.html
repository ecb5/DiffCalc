Problem 5.9.0.7.

(a)
Use your favorite graphing technology to show that each of the following is a parameterization of part of the graph of \(y=x^2\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {3t} {9t^2} {t\gt0}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {-t} {t^2} {t\le0}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {\sqrt{t}} {t} {t\ge0}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {-\sqrt{t}} {t} {t\ge 0}\)
  5. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {1/t} {1/t^2} {\abs{t}\gt 0}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {t^{1/3}} {t^{2/3}} {t\gt 0}\)
  7. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {t^3-1} {t^6-2t^3+1} {-\infty\lt t\lt \infty}\)
  8. \(\displaystyle P(t)= \ParamEqThree {t+1} {t^2+2t+1} {-20\lt t\lt 20}\)
(b)
Sketch only the part of the curve included in each parameterization in part (a). Be sure to indicate the direction of travel in each case, assuming \(t\) is increasing.
(c)
Compute \(\frac{\dot{y}}{\dot{x}}\) and show that this yields \(\dfdx{y}{x}=2x\) for each of the parameterizations in part (a).
