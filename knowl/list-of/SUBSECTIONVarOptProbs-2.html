Problem 9.7.3.1. Variations on a Theme.

(a) Find the Pattern.
  1. Find two positive numbers whose sum is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
  2. Find two positive numbers whose sum is \(8\text{,}\) and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
  3. Find two positive numbers whose sum is \(K\text{,}\) and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
(b) Find the Pattern.
  1. Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and twice the other is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
  2. Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and three times the other is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
  3. Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and five times the other is 10, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
  4. Find two positive numbers such that the sum of one of them and \(n\) times the other is K, and whose product is a maximum. What is the maximum?
(c) Find the Pattern.
  1. Find two positive numbers whose product is \(10\text{,}\) and whose sum is a minimum. What is the minimum?
  2. Find two positive numbers whose product is \(M\text{,}\) and whose sum is a minimum. What is the minimum?
  3. Find two positive numbers whose product is \(M\text{,}\) and the sum of one number and three times the other is a minimum. What is the minimum?
  4. Find two positive numbers whose product is \(M\text{,}\) and the sum of one number and \(n\) times the other is a minimum. What is the minimum?
