Problem 11.2.2.1.

In Problem 7.3.0.8 you showed that the path followed by the center of the rear axle of the trailer follows, \(y=y(t)\text{,}\) must satisfy the IVP:
\begin{align} \dfdx{y}{x}=-\frac{\sqrt{1-x^2}}{x},\amp{}\amp{} y(1)=0.\tag{11.12} \end{align}
(a)
Show that \(y(x)=\ln\left(\frac{1+\sqrt{1-x^2}}{x}\right)-\sqrt{1-x^2}\) satisfies IVP (11.12).
  1. Graph \(y(x)\text{.}\)
  2. Does your graph in part (a) match your intuition about the shape of the curve?
  3. Does it match the graph you found using Euler’s Method in Problem 7.3.0.8?
(b)
How far will the tractor have gone (in trailer lengths) before the trailer is within one degree of vertical?
You will learn how to derive this solution from IVP (11.12) when you take Integral Calculus.
