Problem 9.21. The Largest Triangle with a Fixed Perimeter is Equilateral.
Observe that every triangle is either equilateral, or it is not equilateral.
(a)
Suppose we have a triangle with sides \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\) which is not equilateral. Then at least two sides, say \(a\) and \(c\text{,}\) are not equal. Explain how Problem 9.19 implies that there must be another triangle with the same perimeter and a larger area.
(b)
Use Holmes’ maxim to explain why a triangle with a fixed perimeter enclosing maximal area must be equilateral.
