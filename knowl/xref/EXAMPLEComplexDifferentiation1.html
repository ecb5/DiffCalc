Example 4.38. {\left(\textcolor{red}{\sqrt{x^2-2}}\right)\left(\textcolor{blue}{\sqrt{x}-2}\right)}\right.
To differentiate \(\left( \sqrt{x^2-2} \right) \left( \sqrt{x}-2\right)\) we begin by re-expressing the radicals as exponents because we have no differentiation rules for radicals. \begin{align*} \dx\left[{\left(\textcolor{red}{\sqrt{x^2-2}}\right)\left(\textcolor{blue}{\sqrt{x}-2}\right)}\right] \amp = \dx\left[\left(\textcolor{red}{(x^2-2)^{1/2}}\right)\left(\textcolor{blue}{x^{1/2}-2}\right)\right] \amp = (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\textcolor{blue}{\dx{\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)}}+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\textcolor{red}{\dx{(x^2-2)^{\frac12}}} \amp = (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\left(\frac12x^{-\frac12}\right)\dx{x}+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\left(\frac12(x^2-2)^{-\frac12}\right)\textcolor{red}{\dx(x^2-2)} \amp = (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\left(\frac12x^{-\frac12}\right)\dx{x}+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\left(\frac12(x^2-2)^{-\frac12}\right)2x\dx{x} \amp = \left[ (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\left(\frac12x^{-\frac12}\right)+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\left(\frac12(x^2-2)^{-\frac12}\right)2x\right]\dx{x} \end{align*} Be sure you can follow all the computations at each step in Example Example 4.38. You may want to make some substitutions as you go. With practice, you will be able to do most of this in your head. \begin{ProficiencyDrill} Show that the formula above “simplifies” to
\begin{equation*} \dx\left[\left(\sqrt{x^2-2}\right)\left(\sqrt{x}-2\right)\right] = \frac{3x^2-4x^{\frac32}+2}{2\sqrt{x}\sqrt{x^2-2}}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
Does this seem simpler to you? \end{ProficiencyDrill}
