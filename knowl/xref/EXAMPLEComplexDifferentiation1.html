Example 4.41.

To differentiate \(\left( \sqrt{x^2-2} \right) \left( \sqrt{x}-2\right)\) we begin by re-expressing the radicals as exponents because we have no differentiation rules for radicals.
\begin{align*} \dx{\left(\textcolor{red}{\sqrt{x^2-2}}\right)\amp{}\left(\textcolor{blue}{\sqrt{x}-2}\right)}\\ \amp = \dx\left[\left(\textcolor{red}{(x^2-2)^{1/2}}\right)\left(\textcolor{blue}{x^{1/2}-2}\right)\right]\\ \amp = (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\textcolor{blue}{\dx{\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)}}+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\textcolor{red}{\dx{(x^2-2)^{\frac12}}}\\ \amp = (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\left(\frac12x^{-\frac12}\right)\dx{x}+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\left(\frac12(x^2-2)^{-\frac12}\right)\textcolor{red}{\dx(x^2-2)}\\ \amp = (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\left(\frac12x^{-\frac12}\right)\dx{x}+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\left(\frac12(x^2-2)^{-\frac12}\right)2x\dx{x}\\ \amp = \left[ (x^2-2)^{\frac12}\left(\frac12x^{-\frac12}\right)+\left(x^{\frac12}-2\right)\left(\frac12(x^2-2)^{-\frac12}\right)2x\right]\dx{x} \end{align*}
