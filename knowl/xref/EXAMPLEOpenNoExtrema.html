Example 9.68.
For example, suppose our objective function is \(O(x)=x\text{,}\) and that the domain is the open interval \((-1,1)\text{.}\) Do you see why the graph of this function has no extrema of any kind? If not here is one way to prove it.
\begin{myproof} Observe that \(\dfdx{O}{x}=1\gt 0\text{.}\) Therefore \(O(x)\) is always increasing on \((-1,1)\text{.}\) If \(x\) is any number between \(-1\) and \(1\) then \(f(x)\) can’t be a maximum (minimum) because any number just to the right (left) of \(x\) will give us an \(f(x)\) that is higher (lower) than \(f(x)\text{.}\) Can we always find a number “just to the right” of \(x\) without leaving the interval \((-1,1)?\) Sure. Just take the number halfway between \(x\) and \(1.\) \end{myproof}
