Problem 5.14.

Find an equation of the line(s) tangent to and normal to each of the given curves at the specified points. (You have seen all of these curves previously in Drill 4.51)
(a)
Astroid 2 : \(x^\frac23+y^\frac23 = 4\) at \(\left(1,3\sqrt{3}\right)\) and \(\left(-1,-3\sqrt{3}\right).\)
(b)
An Elliptic Curve: \(y^2=x^3-x+1\) at \((0,1)\) and \((0,-1)\text{.}\)
(c)
Cardioid 3 : \(\left(x^2+y^2\right)^2+4x(x^2+y^2)=4y^2\) at \((0,2)\) and \((0,-2)\text{.}\)
(d)
Conchoid 4  of Nicomedes 5 : \((y-2)^2(x^2+y^2)=2y^2\) at \((1,1)\) and \((3,3).\)
(e)
Cassini 6  oval 7 : \((x^2+y^2+1)^2-4x^2=\frac32\) at \(\left(\frac{\sqrt[4]{2}}{2},\frac{\sqrt[4]{2}}{2}\right)\)
(f)
The Bicorn 8 : \(y^2\left(1-x^2\right)=\left(x^2+2y-1\right)^2\) at \(\left(\frac12, \frac{12\pm3\,\,\sqrt[]{3}}{26}\right)\)
(g)
The Kampyle 9  of Eudoxus 10 : \(x^4=x^2+y^2\) at \(\left(\pm 2, \pm 2\sqrt{3}\right)\)
(h)
The Folium 11 : \((x^2+y^2)(y^2+x(x+4))=4xy^2\) at \(\left(-1, -1\right)\) and \((-1, 1)\)
