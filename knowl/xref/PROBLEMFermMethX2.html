Problem 3.18.

(a)
  1. Use Fermat’s method for tangents to compute the slope of the line tangent to \(y=x^2\) at the generic point \((a,a^2)\text{.}\)
  2. First plot the graph \(y=x^2\text{.}\) Then, on the same set of axes, plot the line through the point \((a,a^2)\text{,}\) and tangent to the graph of \(y=x^2\text{,}\) for each of \(a=-2, -1, 0, 1, 2\text{.}\)
  3. What is the relationship between the slopes at \((a,a^2)\) and \(\left(-a, (-a)^2\right)\text{?}\) Is this consistent with what you see when you plot the graph of \(y=x^2\text{?}\)
(b)
  1. Use Fermat’s method for tangents to compute the slope of the line tangent to \(y=x^3\) at the point \((a,a^3)\text{.}\)
  2. First plot the graph \(y=x^3\text{.}\) Then, on the same set of axes, plot the line through the point \((a,a^3)\text{,}\) and tangent to the graph of \(y=x^3\text{,}\) for each of \(a=-2, -1, 0, 1, 2\text{.}\)You should notice something interesting about the tangent lines at \(\pm1\) and \(\pm2\text{.}\)
  3. What can you say about the tangent lines at \((a, a^3)\) and \(\left(-a, (-a)^3\right)\text{?}\)
