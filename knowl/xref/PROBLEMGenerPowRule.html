Problem 8.96. The General Power Rule.
We are now, finally, in a position to extend the Power Rule to irrational exponents. In fact we’ve already done it.
(a)
Explain how the results of Problem 8.95 imply that if
\begin{equation*} y=x^\alpha{} \end{equation*}
and \(x\gt 0\) then
\begin{equation*} \dx{y}=\alpha x^{\alpha -1}\dx{x} \end{equation*}
even when \(\alpha{}\) is irrational.
(b)
Why did we impose the restriction \(x\gt 0\text{?}\)
Hint.
What happens if \(x\lt 0\) and \(\alpha = 1/2\text{?}\)
