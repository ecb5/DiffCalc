Problem 14.36.
To prove [cross-reference to target(s) "THEOREMthm:PowerRuleLimit" missing or not unique] we will first focus on the special case of \(\beta(x)=x^{\frac{1}{q}}\text{,}\) \(q\) is a non-negative integer. The key to proving this special case is a generalization of the difference of squares formula: \((a-b)(a+b)=a^2-b^2.\)
(a)
Show that \((a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)=a^3-b^3\text{.}\)
(b)
Show that \((a-b)(a^3+a^2b+ab^2+b^3)=a^4-b^4\text{.}\)
(c)
Use \term{Mathematical Induction} to show that\\ \centerline{\((a-b)(a^{q-1}+a^{q-2}b+a^{q-3}b^2+\cdots+ab^{q-2}+b^{q-1})=a^q-b^q\text{.}\)}
