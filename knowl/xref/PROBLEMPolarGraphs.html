Problem 6.21.
The graph of an equation given in polar coordinates is quite different from one given in Cartesian coordinates. To get a sense of this, sketch the graph of each of the following functions in polar coordinates 3 .
  1. \(\displaystyle r=\sin(\theta)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle r=\cos(\theta)\)
  3. \(\displaystyle r=\sin(2\theta)\)
  4. \(\displaystyle r=\sin(3\theta)\)
  5. \(\displaystyle r=1+\cos(\theta)\)
  6. \(\displaystyle r=\sin(\theta)\cos(\theta)\)
  7. \(\displaystyle r=1\)
  8. \(\displaystyle \theta=\pi/4\)
  9. \(\displaystyle r=\sin(\theta)+\cos(\theta)\)
  10. \(\displaystyle r=\tan(\theta)\)
  11. \(\displaystyle r=\sec(\theta)\)
  12. \(\displaystyle r=\sin(\theta)-\cos(\theta)\)
