Problem 6.44.
Let \(\rho\) be a point on a circle of radius \(a\) that is rolling on the \(x\)-axis and let \(P(\theta)= \begin{ParamEq} {x(\theta)} {y(\theta)} \end{ParamEq}\) be the position of \(\rho\) when the ball has rolled through an angle of \(\theta\text{.}\) \centerline{\includegraphics*[height=1.6in,width=3.8in]{../Figures/Cycloid2}}
(a)
  1. Use the diagram above to show that the parameterization of \(P(\theta)\) is given by
    \begin{equation*} \label{eq:CycloidParameterization} P(\theta) = \begin{ParamEq} {x(\theta)} {y(\theta)} {-\infty\lt \theta \lt \infty} \end{ParamEq} = \begin{ParamEq} {a\theta-a\sin(\theta)} {a-a\cos(\theta)} {-\infty\lt \theta \lt \infty} \end{ParamEq}. \end{equation*}
  2. Compute \(\dx{x}\) and \(\dx{y}\) in terms of \(\dx{\theta}.\)
  3. Find the slope of the tangent line to the cycloid at an arbitrary point \(P\) in terms of \(\theta\) and use this to confirm that the tangent line is horizontal at the apex of the cycloid, point \(P\) in our diagram.
  4. Show that
    \begin{equation*} \dfdx{y}{x} =\frac{1+\cos{(\theta)}}{\sin(\theta)}, \end{equation*}
    and use this to examine the slope of the cycloid near the points where it touches the \(x\)-axis.
