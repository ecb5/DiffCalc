Theorem 15.10. First Derivative Test.

Suppose \(f(x)\) is continuous on the interval \([\alpha,\beta]\text{,}\) and differentiable on the interval \((\alpha,\beta)\text{.}\) Suppose further that both \(a\) and \(b\) are in the interval \([\alpha,\beta]\) and \(b\gt a\text{.}\)
  1. If \(f^\prime(x)\gt 0\) on the interval \((\alpha,\beta)\) then \(f(b)\gt f(a)\text{.}\) (That is, the function is increasing on \([\alpha,\beta]\text{.}\))
  2. If \(f^\prime(x)\lt 0\) on the interval \((\alpha,\beta)\) then \(f(b)\lt f(a)\text{.}\) (That is, the function is decreasing on \([\alpha,\beta]\text{.}\))
